Okra

Abelmoschus esculentus
Clemson Spineless
A popular, high-yielding variety that produces dark green, spineless pods. A staple in Southern cuisine, great for frying, grilling, or in gumbos.
Plant Care ▼
Sunlight
Full Sun (8+ hours/day)
Watering
Drought-tolerant once established, but consistent watering yields better pods. Water deeply once a week.
Soil
Prefers fertile, well-drained soil. pH 6.5-7.0.
Fertilizing
Mix compost into the soil before planting. Side-dress with fertilizer if leaves turn pale green.
Temperature
75-90°F (24-32°C)
Spacing
18 inches apart
Growing Timeline ▼
5-10
Days to Germinate
50-60
Days to Harvest
Harvesting Tips
Harvest pods when they are 2-4 inches long. They become tough if left longer. Use a knife or pruners to cut the stem.
Health & Troubleshooting ▼

Common Pests

Aphids Stink Bugs Corn Earworms

Common Diseases

Powdery Mildew Fusarium Wilt

Troubleshooting

Yellow, sticky pods are overripe. Lack of pods can be due to extreme heat or lack of pollination.
Additional Information ▼

Fun Fact

Okra is related to hibiscus and cotton.
